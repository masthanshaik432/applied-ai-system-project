# Heart Disease in Pets

Cardiac conditions (e.g., dilated cardiomyopathy, mitral valve disease) reduce the heart's ability to pump blood efficiently. Exercise tolerance is significantly limited.

## Exercise Guidelines
- Strenuous or prolonged walks are contraindicated. Brief, calm outings (5–10 minutes) for bathroom needs only are appropriate.
- Avoid exercise in hot or humid weather — cardiovascular strain is higher.
- Watch for exercise intolerance signs: coughing, rapid breathing, weakness, collapsing. Stop immediately and consult a vet.
- Excitement and stress should be minimized; sudden bursts of activity can trigger cardiac events.

## Medication and Scheduling
- Cardiac medications (e.g., furosemide, enalapril, pimobendan) must be given on a strict schedule — often twice daily, 12 hours apart.
- Timing consistency is critical: irregular dosing leads to unstable drug levels and can worsen symptoms rapidly.
- Never skip a cardiac medication dose. If a dose is missed, give it as soon as possible unless the next dose is within 2 hours.

## Priority Notes
- Medication tasks for cardiac pets are the highest priority in any daily plan. Mark as inflexible.
- Walk tasks should be very short, flexible, and lowest priority. They can be replaced by a brief indoor movement session if the pet is having a bad day.

# Obesity in Pets

Obesity is one of the most common and preventable health problems in dogs and cats. Excess weight puts strain on joints, the heart, and the respiratory system, and shortens life expectancy.

## Feeding Guidelines
- Measure meals precisely — free feeding (leaving food out all day) makes calorie control impossible.
- Split daily food allowance into two or more meals rather than one large meal; this reduces hunger, stabilizes blood sugar, and discourages bolting food.
- Avoid high-calorie treats; substitute with low-calorie alternatives (baby carrots for dogs, plain cooked chicken for cats) if needed.
- Schedule feeding times at consistent intervals (e.g., 7 AM and 6 PM) to regulate appetite and metabolism.

## Exercise Guidelines
- Regular, moderate exercise is strongly beneficial for weight loss — do not skip walks for obese dogs.
- Start with shorter sessions (15–20 minutes) and increase duration gradually as fitness improves.
- Avoid exercise immediately after meals; wait at least 30 minutes.
- For cats, scheduled play sessions (feather wand, laser pointer) replace walks as the exercise mechanism.

## Priority Notes
- Feeding tasks should be inflexible and scheduled at consistent times.
- Walk or play tasks should be high priority despite the obesity condition — exercise is part of treatment, not a luxury.

# General Cat Care Guidelines

## Feeding
- Adult cats should be fed twice daily at consistent times; free feeding is not recommended for weight management.
- Cats are obligate carnivores — ensure the diet is high in animal protein.
- Fresh water must always be available; many cats prefer running water (consider a fountain).
- Avoid sudden diet changes — transition new foods over 7–10 days to prevent GI upset.

## Enrichment and Play
- Indoor cats require daily interactive play to prevent boredom, obesity, and behavioral issues.
- Schedule at least two 10–15 minute interactive play sessions per day (morning and evening are ideal).
- Rotate toys to maintain novelty; feather wands, laser pointers, and puzzle feeders are highly effective.
- Provide vertical space (cat trees, shelves) for climbing and territory expression.

## Grooming
- Short-haired cats: brush weekly to reduce shedding and hairballs.
- Long-haired cats (Persian, Maine Coon): brush daily to prevent matting.
- Cats generally self-groom but benefit from owner brushing for bonding and early detection of skin issues.
- Nail trims every 2–3 weeks for indoor cats.
- Dental care: brush teeth 2–3 times per week or use dental treats/water additives.

## Scheduling Priority
- Feeding tasks are inflexible and highest priority.
- Play/enrichment sessions are high priority for indoor cats — treat as non-optional for wellbeing.
- Grooming is flexible but should be consistent, especially for long-haired breeds.

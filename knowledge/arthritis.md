# Arthritis in Pets

Arthritis (osteoarthritis) is a degenerative joint condition common in older dogs and cats. It causes chronic pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility, especially after rest or in cold weather.

## Exercise Guidelines
- Avoid high-impact or prolonged walks. Short, gentle walks (10–15 minutes) on flat, soft surfaces are preferred over long or strenuous outings.
- Swimming or hydrotherapy is an excellent low-impact alternative where available.
- Never skip movement entirely — total inactivity causes muscle loss and worsens joint stiffness. Light daily movement is better than sporadic long sessions.
- Schedule walks during the warmest part of the day when joints are less stiff.
- Watch for signs of pain after activity: limping, reluctance to rise, whimpering. Shorten the next session if these appear.

## Medication and Scheduling
- NSAIDs (e.g., carprofen, meloxicam) should be given consistently with food to reduce GI irritation.
- Do not skip medication doses — consistent plasma levels are more effective than on-demand dosing.
- Joint supplements (glucosamine, chondroitin, omega-3s) are often prescribed alongside medication; schedule them at the same time each day to build habit.

## Priority Notes
- Medication tasks for arthritic pets should be treated as high priority (non-flexible, non-skippable).
- Walk tasks should be marked flexible and lower priority than medication; they can be shortened or rescheduled if the pet shows discomfort.

# General Dog Care Guidelines

## Daily Exercise
- Most adult dogs need 30–60 minutes of exercise per day, split into at least two outings.
- Puppies (under 1 year) should follow the "5-minute rule": 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice daily, to protect developing joints.
- Senior dogs (7+ years) may need shorter but more frequent walks; watch for fatigue.
- Mental stimulation (sniff walks, puzzle feeders) is as important as physical exercise for behavioral health.

## Feeding
- Adult dogs should be fed twice daily (morning and evening) at consistent times.
- Puppies under 6 months require three meals per day.
- Always provide fresh water; refill morning and evening.
- Avoid feeding within 1 hour before or after vigorous exercise to reduce bloat risk (especially in deep-chested breeds).

## Grooming
- Short-coated breeds: brush weekly, bathe monthly or as needed.
- Long-coated or double-coated breeds: brush 3–4 times per week to prevent matting, bathe every 4–6 weeks.
- Nail trims every 3–4 weeks; overgrown nails alter gait and cause joint strain.
- Ear checks weekly; clean as needed to prevent infection.
- Dental brushing daily or at minimum 3 times per week.

## Scheduling Priority
- Feeding is non-negotiable and inflexible — always highest priority.
- Exercise is high priority for physical and mental health; only deprioritize if a health condition restricts it.
- Grooming is flexible and can be rescheduled, but should not be skipped indefinitely.

# Post-Surgery Recovery in Pets

Recovery requirements depend on the type of surgery, but most soft-tissue and orthopedic procedures share common restrictions to prevent wound dehiscence, infection, and re-injury.

## Activity Restrictions
- Strict rest is required for the first 10–14 days following most surgeries. No running, jumping, or rough play.
- Leash walks are limited to short bathroom breaks (2–3 minutes) only; no free roaming outdoors.
- Stairs should be avoided or minimized; use ramps or carry the pet if necessary.
- Grooming near the surgical site is strictly contraindicated until sutures are removed (typically 10–14 days post-op). Full grooming sessions should be postponed.
- The e-collar (cone) must remain on except during supervised eating; prevent licking or chewing at the incision.

## Medication
- Pain medication and antibiotics are typically prescribed post-operatively. Both must be completed on the prescribed schedule.
- Do not stop antibiotics early even if the pet appears recovered — incomplete courses contribute to resistance and risk of re-infection.
- Anti-nausea medication may be prescribed for the first 24–48 hours; give with a small amount of food if permitted.

## Priority Notes
- Medication tasks are the top priority post-surgery; mark as inflexible.
- Walk tasks should be very short and flagged with low priority. Full walks should not be scheduled during the recovery window.
- Grooming tasks should be removed entirely until cleared by the veterinarian.

# Medication Administration Guidelines

## General Principles
- Consistency is the most important factor in medication effectiveness. Give medications at the same time each day to maintain stable therapeutic drug levels in the bloodstream.
- Never skip doses — for most medications, irregular dosing reduces efficacy and may cause rebound symptoms or resistance.
- If a dose is missed: give it as soon as you remember, unless the next scheduled dose is within 2 hours. In that case, skip the missed dose and resume the normal schedule. Never double-dose.

## With or Without Food
- Most medications should be given with food to reduce nausea and GI irritation (NSAIDs, antibiotics, steroids).
- Some medications require an empty stomach for proper absorption — always follow veterinary instructions.
- Pill pockets, cream cheese, or a small amount of wet food are effective for disguising tablets.

## Timing Recommendations
- Once-daily medications: same time each day, tied to a consistent routine event (e.g., morning feeding).
- Twice-daily medications: ideally 12 hours apart (e.g., 7 AM and 7 PM) to maintain even drug levels.
- Three-times-daily: space doses 8 hours apart.

## Scheduling Priority
- Medication tasks should always be marked as non-flexible and high priority (4–5).
- Scheduling medication alongside or just after feeding tasks is ideal — it reinforces the habit and most medications are given with food.
- Never schedule medication tasks after a large block of flexible or low-priority tasks; they should appear early and prominently in the daily plan.
